Budget Tips

Saving Money in Japan

Your big-ticket items are locked in at great rates. Here's how to keep daily spending lean without missing out on anything.

$1,500–$2,000
Estimated savings if you follow these tips across the whole trip. That's enough for a couple of fancy dinners and a shopping spree at Don Quijote.
🚇

Getting Around for Less

JR Kansai-Hiroshima Area Pass

This 5-day pass covers unlimited Shinkansen between Shin-Osaka and Hiroshima, plus all local JR trains and the Miyajima ferry. At ~$175 AUD per adult vs ~$225+ buying individual bullet train tickets, it's a no-brainer for your Osaka → Hiroshima → Kyoto leg.

Bronte (under 12) gets a half-price child pass.

Save ~$250+ for the family

Hokkaido Expressway Pass (HEP)

Highway tolls in Japan are notoriously steep. When you pick up the Alphard, ask for the HEP — it gives unlimited use of Hokkaido's expressways for a flat daily fee. On your Furano → Biei → Asahikawa → Otaru → Sapporo route, this will almost certainly save you money and definitely save hassle.

Save ~$100–150

Smart-EX App (Early Bird Shinkansen)

For the Kyoto → Nagoya leg, download the Smart-EX app before you leave. Book 21–28 days ahead to access "Hayatoku" early-bird fares. It also lets you pick seats and change reservations easily from your phone.

Save 10–20% per ticket

IC Cards (Suica / ICOCA)

Get one per person at any station. Tap-on/tap-off for trains, buses, and vending machines. Fares are actually a few yen cheaper than paper tickets. Load them at any convenience store or station machine. They work nationwide across Osaka, Kyoto, Hiroshima, and Sapporo.

Convenience + small savings daily
🍲

Eating Smart

Lunch is the Fancy Meal

If you want to try Hida beef, high-end sushi, or omakase, do it at lunch. Most restaurants offer teishoku (set meals) that are 30–50% cheaper than dinner for the same quality. A ¥5,000 dinner dish often appears as a ¥2,500 lunch set.

Save 30–50% on splurge meals

Konbini Breakfasts

Skip paid hotel breakfasts (unless included). 7-Eleven, Lawson, and FamilyMart have genuinely great egg sandwiches, onigiri, pastries, and iced coffee. Budget about $4–6 AUD per person — the whole family eats for under $25.

Save ~$30–50/day vs hotel breakfast

Supermarket Evenings

You have kitchens in Kyoto (Airbnb) and Furano (house). Hit a local supermarket (Life, Fresco, or Aeon) after 7:30 PM when they discount fresh bento, sushi, and deli items by up to 50%. You can feed everyone a feast for under $30 AUD.

Save ~$50–70 per evening

Family Restaurants (Famiresu)

Chains like Gusto, Saizeriya, Royal Host, and Joyfull are lifesavers for families. They serve a mix of Japanese and Western food, cost $8–12 AUD per meal, and have unlimited "drink bars" for a couple of dollars. Great for nights when everyone's tired.

Reliable & cheap family dinners

Bring Reusable Water Bottles

Tap water in Japan is perfectly safe. Buying 3–4 vending machine drinks per day for 5 people adds up to over $30 AUD daily. Fill up at your accommodation before heading out. Most train stations and parks have water fountains too.

Save ~$500+ over the whole trip
🏭

Activities & Shopping

Tax-Free Shopping

Japan offers 10% tax-free shopping for tourists on purchases over ¥5,000 (~$50 AUD) at the same store. Always carry your physical passports. Places like Don Quijote (Donki) and department stores all offer it — just ask at the register.

Save 10% on shopping

Check Klook Before You Queue

Before walking up to the Kaiyukan Aquarium, Osaka Castle, Sapporo ropeways, etc., check the Klook app. They often have family bundles or slight discounts compared to the gate price, and you skip the ticket queues too.

Save 5–15% + skip queues

Free Stuff is Amazing in Japan

Some of the best experiences in Japan are free: shrine and temple grounds (Fushimi Inari, Meiji Shrine), public parks, department store food-floor tastings, walking through Arashiyama bamboo grove, Dotonbori at night, and most festivals. Build these into your days alongside ticketed activities.

💼

Luggage Shipping (Takkyubin)

Consolidate Before Shipping

You don't need to ship all 5 suitcases every time. Pack a shared carry-on or backpacks with 2–3 days' worth of clothes and ship only 2–3 large bags. At ~¥2,500 per bag per shipment, going from 5 bags to 3 saves you about $50–75 AUD each time. Over 3 shipments that's $150–225.

Save ~$150–225 total
📍

Location-Specific Tips

Osaka (3 nights)

Osaka Amazing Pass

If you plan to visit Osaka Castle, the Kaiyukan Aquarium, and take a river cruise, the 1-day Amazing Pass (~¥2,800) bundles free entry to 50+ attractions plus unlimited subway. Can easily save ¥3,000–4,000 per person per day.

Worth it if doing 3+ attractions

Shinsekai for Cheap Eats

Kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers) in Shinsekai are ridiculously cheap — sets start from ¥500. It's a great family dinner option that also doubles as a sightseeing experience with the retro Tsutenkaku Tower lit up above you.

Hiroshima (2 nights)

Miyajima Ferry is Covered

If you get the JR Kansai-Hiroshima Pass, the JR ferry to Miyajima Island is included. No need to buy a separate ferry ticket — just flash the pass.

Ferry already included in JR Pass

Hiroshima Okonomiyaki

Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki is a must-try and extremely cheap — a full meal for ¥800–1,200 per person. Head to Okonomimura (Okonomiyaki Village), a multi-floor building packed with stalls.

Kyoto & Nara (4 nights)

Most Temples Have Free Grounds

Fushimi Inari (the famous torii gates) is completely free. Many temples charge only ¥300–500 to enter the inner gardens. Pick 2–3 "must-see" paid temples and enjoy the free grounds of the rest.

Nishiki Market for Tastings

Walk through Nishiki Market and sample free tastings as you go — pickles, mochi, tea, soy milk donuts. It's a meal in itself and a great activity with the family.

Takayama (3 nights)

Ryokan Meals = Big Saving

Your Ouan Ryokan includes breakfast and dinner. That's 3 nights of meals for everyone already covered — a massive saving given you'd be spending ¥5,000–8,000 per adult otherwise. Take advantage!

~$400–600 in meals already included

Morning Markets

Takayama's famous morning markets (Jinya-mae and Miyagawa) run daily. Great for cheap local snacks, fresh fruit, and unique souvenirs at lower prices than touristy shops.

Hokkaido & Sapporo (8 nights)

Cook in Furano

Your Furano house has a kitchen. Hit up an A-Coop or local farm stand for incredibly fresh Hokkaido produce, dairy, and meats. Hokkaido corn, melons, and dairy are famous across Japan — cook with them!

Save ¥5,000+/night vs eating out

Sapporo Ramen Alley

A bowl of miso ramen in Sapporo costs ¥800–1,000 — one of the cheapest quality meals you'll find. Ramen Yokocho (Ramen Alley) has a great atmosphere and the portions are generous.
