Pre-Trip To-Do List

Click any checkbox to mark it done. Your progress is saved in your browser.

  • Check passports are valid 6+ months past return

    Japan requires passports valid for the duration of stay. Check all 5 passports (including Bronte's) — renewal takes 2–3 weeks in Australia.

    Do now

  • Set up Wise cards (Matt + parents)

    Order a Wise card for yourself and one for Mum & Dad. Pre-load with JPY when the rate looks good. See the Money section below for details.

    Do now

  • Buy JR Kansai-Hiroshima Area Pass

    5-day pass for Osaka → Hiroshima → Kyoto leg. Buy online in advance from JR West — it's slightly cheaper than buying at the station. 4 adults + 1 child pass.

    Book soon

  • Download Smart-EX app & register

    For booking the Kyoto → Nagoya Shinkansen. Register your credit card and passport details. Book 21–28 days before travel for Hayatoku early-bird fares.

    Book soon

  • Check Klook for activity bundles

    Look up Osaka Amazing Pass, Kaiyukan Aquarium, Sapporo ropeway tickets, etc. Pre-booking can save 5–15% and skip queues.

    Book soon

  • Arrange travel insurance

    Make sure it covers the whole family including parents. Japan has excellent healthcare but it's expensive without insurance. Check your credit card — some offer complimentary cover.

    Book soon

  • Register on Visit Japan Web

    Japan's official entry portal. Fill in immigration and customs declarations online before you fly — generates a QR code for faster arrival. Register all 5 travellers.

    2 weeks before

  • Research Takkyubin (luggage forwarding)

    Yamato Transport (Kuroneko) is the most popular. Hotels and convenience stores can arrange it. Plan which legs you'll ship bags vs carry them — consolidate to save money.

    2 weeks before

  • Sort mobile data (eSIM or pocket WiFi)

    Get an eSIM (Ubigi, Airalo, or Mobal) for each phone, or rent a pocket WiFi to share. eSIMs are cheapest at ~$15–25 AUD for 21 days. Order/activate before departure.

    2 weeks before

  • Withdraw some JPY cash before leaving

    Bring ¥30,000–50,000 per adult for the first few days. You'll top up at 7-Eleven ATMs in Japan, but it's good to land with cash for the train to your hotel.

    1 week before

  • Get IC cards (Suica/ICOCA) on arrival

    Buy one per person at the airport or first train station. Load ¥2,000–3,000 each to start. Used for trains, buses, vending machines, and convenience stores nationwide.

    On arrival

  • Ask for Hokkaido Expressway Pass at car rental

    When picking up the Alphard in Hokkaido, request the HEP for unlimited expressway use. Saves money on tolls for the Furano → Biei → Otaru → Sapporo route.

    At car pickup
