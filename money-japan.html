Practical Guide

Money in Japan

How to pay, where to withdraw, and the smartest cards to bring for your family.

The Short Version

Japan has gone increasingly cashless, but cash is still essential for small shops, temples, markets, and rural areas (especially Takayama and Hokkaido countryside).

Your best combo: Wise card for everyday spending + cash for places that don't take cards + IC card for transit.

💰

Payment Methods

💳 Wise Card

The best option for your family. Wise uses the real mid-market exchange rate with a small transparent fee (typically 0.4–0.6% for AUD → JPY). No monthly fees, no surprises.

  • Pre-load JPY when the rate looks good
  • Set rate alerts in the app to auto-convert
  • Works at most Japanese shops and restaurants
  • Withdraw cash from any 7-Eleven ATM (¥100 fee)
  • One-time $10 AUD card order fee, then free

For your parents: Since they're coming to Australia first, help them set up a Wise account and order a card while they're here. Load it with JPY together so they're comfortable using it. The app is simple and has good multi-language support.

🏪 Cash (Still Important)

Despite the cashless trend, you'll need cash more than you'd expect. Budget about 40% of daily spending as cash.

  • Temples, shrines, and small shops are cash-only
  • Takayama morning markets — cash only
  • Some restaurants, especially in Hokkaido rural areas
  • Vending machines (some take IC cards, many don't)
  • Coin lockers at train stations

Where to withdraw: 7-Eleven ATMs (Seven Bank) are everywhere and accept international cards. Japan Post ATMs are another reliable option.

🚃 IC Cards (Suica / ICOCA)

A must-have for transit. Tap on/off for trains and buses. Also works at convenience stores, vending machines, and many chain restaurants.

  • ¥500 deposit + load amount (refundable)
  • Fares are slightly cheaper than paper tickets
  • Top up at any station or convenience store
  • Works nationwide: Osaka, Kyoto, Hiroshima, Sapporo
  • Get Welcome Suica at airport (28-day tourist version)

Tip: Apple Pay users can add a mobile Suica — no physical card needed. Top up directly from your Wise card.

💳 Regular Bank Cards

Your normal Australian debit/credit cards work in Japan, but fees add up fast.

  • Foreign transaction fee: 2–3%
  • Currency conversion markup: 1–3%
  • ATM fee: $3–8 per withdrawal
  • Combined: 4–6% more than Wise

Keep a regular card as backup, but use Wise for primary spending. On a $10,000 spend, Wise saves you $400–600 in fees.

💡

Key Money Tips

Always Choose JPY at Terminals

When a card machine asks "pay in AUD or JPY?" — always pick JPY. Paying in AUD triggers "Dynamic Currency Conversion" with terrible rates (3–5% markup).

Carry Small Denominations

Many ticket machines and smaller vending machines don't take ¥10,000 notes. Break big notes at convenience stores or when buying train tickets.

Tax-Free Shopping (免税)

Spend ¥5,000+ at one store? Ask for tax-free. Show your passport. That's 10% off instantly on souvenirs, cosmetics, and clothes. Don Quijote and department stores all offer it.

Daily Budget Estimate

At mid-range spending: ~¥25,000–35,000/day for all 5 of you (~$230–320 AUD). Higher in cities, lower in Takayama where ryokan meals are included.
